Computational Scientist

Patrick Zulian, PhD

Patrick Zulian, is a scientific researcher and lecturer at the Euler institute, Università della Svizzera italiana (USI), and at UniDistance Suisse. His work ranges from domain decomposition methods for multi-physics simulations to developing algorithms for large-scale scientific computing. He is the creator and lead developer of several software libraries in collaboration with the Swiss National Computing Center (CSCS) and a contributor to international open-source projects, such as MFEM (Lawrence Livermore National Lab, LLNL). He is the Principal investigator (PI) and co-PI on computational multi-physics projects with a strong focus on algorithms and high-performance computing. He received a Ph.D. in computational science from USI in 2017 and has a computer science and software engineering background.

Selected Scientific Achievements

Coupling Techniques for Multiphysics

The developed parallel variational resampling strategies between non-matching meshes are the core component of a large number of scientific contributions, involving local partners at HSLU, ETHZ, UniBern, and USI, and international partners at LLNL. The developed techniques combine geometric search, load balancing, and construction or variational operators.

Immersed Fluid-Structure (Contact) Interaction

Fully coupled fluid and structural physics using immersed boundary and domain techniques. Collaborative efforts to couple different fluid discretizations, including FDM, FVM and FEM with nonlinear elastic structures. Recent extensions include the resolution of contact problems between the immersed hyper-elastic structures.

Computational Geophysics Tooling

Advanced mixed-dimensional models and softwarefor fractured porous media using dual Lagrange multipliers, for fracture network simulations in geothermal applications. Techniques have been analyzed and assessed for different discretization orders and types of Lagrange multipliers. This work has been carried out in collaboration with ETHZ and collaborators at USI.

Uncertainty Quantification at Scale

Hierarchical PDE samplers for multilevel Monte Carlo analyses, generating spatially correlated random fields over general domains to assess model sensitivity and risk. This work has been carried out in collaboration with partners at the Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory (LLNL).

Collaborative Research Networks

Interdisciplinary collaborations with LLNL on uncertainty quantification, ETH Zürich on geophysics, University of Bern on biomedical applications, University of Turin on numerical methods, HSLU and KNF FLodos AG on industrial application on liquid diaphragm pumps, and the Swiss National Supercomputing Centre to devise algorithms and implement them into HPC software.
