Computational Scientist

Patrick Zulian, PhD

Patrick Zulian, is a scientific researcher and lecturer at the Euler institute, Università della Svizzera italiana (USI), and at UniDistance Suisse. His work ranges from domain decomposition methods for multi-physics simulations to developing algorithms for large-scale scientific computing. He is the creator and lead developer of several software libraries in collaboration with the Swiss National Computing Center (CSCS) and a contributor to international open-source projects, such as MFEM (Lawrence Livermore National Lab, LLNL). He is the Principal investigator (PI) and co-PI on computational multi-physics projects with a strong focus on algorithms and high-performance computing. He received a Ph.D. in computational science from USI in 2017 and has a computer science and software engineering background.

About

I develop scalable numerical methods and software for multiphysics simulations, with a focus on fluid-structure interaction, non-conforming mesh techniques, and uncertainty quantification. My work bridges algorithm research with production-level HPC implementations, delivering tools for geophysics, biomedicine, and industrial partners.

Selected Scientific Achievements

Variational Transfer for Multiphysics

Created parallel variational resampling strategies to transfer discrete fields between non-matching meshes, combining geometric search, load balancing, and operator construction. The software underpins coupled simulations across geophysics and biomedical applications.

Read the paper

Fluid-Structure Interaction Frameworks

Developed complementary FSI frameworks: a finite element and high-order finite difference coupling for fluid and structural physics, and an all-finite-element approach capable of simulating contact between immersed hyper-elastic structures.

Framework details

Computational Geophysics Tooling

Advanced mixed-dimensional models for fractured porous media using dual Lagrange multipliers, and released software that drives fracture network simulations for geothermal applications.

Representative work

Space-Time Multilevel Methods

Designed scalable multigrid solvers and adaptive mesh managers for space-time finite element discretizations, enabling efficient 4D simulations with automatic coarse level generation.

Explore the methods

Uncertainty Quantification at Scale

Co-developed hierarchical PDE samplers for multilevel Monte Carlo analyses, generating spatially correlated random fields over general domains to assess model sensitivity and risk.

Learn more

Collaborative Research Networks

Led interdisciplinary collaborations with LLNL, ETH Zürich, University of Bern, University of Turin, HSLU, KNF Flodos AG, and the Swiss National Supercomputing Centre to translate algorithms into production-ready HPC software.
